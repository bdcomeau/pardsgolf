🚀 Quick Start — Do These First

A handful of one-time settings make every round after this one faster and more accurate. Do these in order the first time you open the app:

  • Have your Golf Canada username and password ready. You'll be asked once the first time you fetch any handicap, and the app remembers it after that — no need to log in again on this device.
  • Set yourself as "Me." In ⚙️ Manage My Players, find your name (or add yourself) and mark yourself as "Me." This auto-fills you as Player 1 every round and unlocks the personal settings below.
  • Set your Home City. Still in Manage My Players, on your "Me" entry. This drives the live weather shown on the Setup screen every time you open the app — leave blank or "Edmonton" for a personal WeatherLink station, or set any other city for its own live Open-Meteo weather.
  • Set your Home Course. Also on your "Me" entry. Whatever course you pick always shows up first in the course picker and is selected automatically the moment you start a new round — no more re-selecting Derrick (or wherever you actually play) every single time.
  • Set your preferred tee. Your Derrick tee deck (Deck 1 through Deck 4) is the reference point the app uses to automatically pick the closest-matching tee for you at every other course in the database — no manual tee-hunting needed at courses you've never played in the app before. You can always override any individual course's pick later.
  • Add your regular players and their typical score. Save your regular group as contacts, star your most frequent playing partners as Favourites (tap "⭐ Load My Favourites" to auto-fill them every round), and set each player's "Score Wheel Default" (Par, Bogey, or Double) so the score entry wheel always opens centred on the number they actually shoot.
  • Fetch everyone's handicap. Once everyone has a Golf Canada ID on file, tap "🔄 Refresh All Handicaps" in Manage My Players to pull every saved player's current Index in one batch — no need to fetch each person one at a time.

That's it — every round after this is just picking a course (or letting your Home Course pick itself), adding players, and starting. See "Manage My Players" for the full detail on every setting above.

Don't be surprised to see dozens of names already in Manage My Players the first time you open the app — a starter roster ships with the app and merges in safely by Golf Canada ID. It only ever adds players who aren't already on your device; it never overwrites or duplicates anyone you've already saved or customized, even across future app updates.

Privacy & Data Security

Everything stays on your device

All round data, player names, handicaps, betting results, and Golf Canada member IDs are stored exclusively in your iPhone's browser storage (localStorage). This data never leaves your device and is never sent to any server, cloud service, or third party.

Golf Canada Member ID & Password

Your Golf Canada credentials are used only to fetch your current handicap index. They are stored only on your device, never transmitted to anyone other than Golf Canada's own secure API, and never included in exports or backups.

Pards Golf does not collect analytics, does not serve ads, has no user accounts, and has no server. Your data is yours alone.

1. Installing Pards Golf on Your iPhone

Pards Golf is a Progressive Web App (PWA). Add it to your Home Screen directly from Safari — not from the App Store.

You MUST install to your Home Screen before your data will be reliably saved. Opening in a browser tab means your data can be cleared at any time.
Do NOT use "Remove App" on the Home Screen icon — this permanently wipes all local data.

Step-by-Step Installation

1
Open Safari on your iPhone.
2
Go to: https://bdcomeau.github.io/pardsgolf/pards-golf.html
3
Tap the Share button (square with arrow pointing up, bottom toolbar).
4
Tap "Add to Home Screen."
5
Tap "Add" in the top-right corner.
6
Always launch from the Home Screen icon — not from a Safari bookmark.

Keeping the App Current

Tap Update App in the bottom action bar to check for updates. If still outdated, force-close and reopen, or clear Safari site data for bdcomeau.github.io (Settings → Safari → Advanced → Website Data).

Do NOT delete the Home Screen icon to update — this permanently erases all local data.

2. Setting Up a Round

Step 1 — Choose Your Course

Tap the course picker at the top. Your Home Course (set in Manage My Players) always appears first in the list. If no home course is set, Derrick is first by default. Tap 📋 Info on any course row to see live weather, your history there, and the full scorecard.

Step 2 — Set Number of Players

Use + and − to set 1 to 5 players.

Step 3 — Load Your Favourites

Tap ⭐ Load My Favourites to fill in your saved regular group automatically.

Step 4 — Adding a Player

Tap the Select Saved Player button to open a searchable list of your saved contacts — sorted with "Me" first, then ⭐ Favourites, then alphabetically. Each row shows their handicap index and lifetime money. Or type a new name directly:

  • Type their name, enter their handicap index, select their tee.
  • Optionally enter their 10-digit Golf Canada Member ID.
  • Tap 💾 Save to save as a contact for future rounds.

Step 5 — Fetch Handicaps from Golf Canada

If a Golf Canada ID is entered, tap the Fetch button on their row. First fetch asks for their password once — remembered for all future fetches.

Step 6 — Starting Hole

Defaults to Hole 1. Change for shotgun starts.

Step 7 — Scores Only or With Games?

  • With Games (default) — all betting games available.
  • Scores Only — no betting games.

Step 8 — Birdie Juice Tracking

Toggle 🍺 Birdie Juice Tracking on or off — turn off for rounds with non-drinkers.

Step 9 — Configure Betting Games

Scroll to Betting Games. Tap any accordion header to expand. A green ✓ Active label appears on headers where a game is configured. Pairing pills show full game detail (e.g. "Nassau 2v2 · $2/$2/$2 + $4 Auto Press").

Step 10 — Start the Round

Tap the gold Start Round → button pinned to the bottom of the screen.

Before any score is entered, the scorecard button says "✏️ Edit Round Settings" — tap it to go back to Setup.

3. Scoring — The Scorecard Screen

Entering Scores

Scroll each player's wheel to their score, then tap the centre to confirm. A birdie or better triggers a Birdie Juice popup (if enabled). Correct any score by scrolling again — everything recalculates instantly.

The Settings Button

  • ✏️ Edit Round Settings — before any score is entered. Returns to Setup.
  • ⚙️ Mid-Round Match & Player Settings — once any score is entered. Opens mid-round overlay.

Mid-Round Settings

Change handicaps, switch tee boxes (tap their coloured tee pill), add/remove players, add/adjust any betting game, change starting hole, or toggle Auto-Advance.

Any game can be added mid-round and will back-calculate results for holes already played.
Change the starting hole mid-round (handy for a shotgun start correction) and the app doesn't just reset — it actually shifts every score you've already entered to its correct new position, shows you exactly which holes are moving where before you confirm, and even shifts Snake 3-putt history along with it.
Switch a player's tee box mid-round and everything recalculates instantly: their handicap strokes for every remaining hole, and every Nassau match they're part of re-zeroes its relative handicaps in case the tee change shifted who the lowest-handicap player in the match is.

4. Nassau Games

A three-part bet — Front 9, Back 9, Total 18 — each worth one bet unit. Auto-presses tracked automatically.

2v2 Nassau (High-Low)

Two teams of two. Each team contributes their Low ball and High ball net score. Up to three simultaneous 2v2 pairings with 4 players. A gold checkmark (✓) appears next to whichever player's score "counted" for their team on each hole — and that checkmark carries through to every PDF, print, and shared export too, not just the live screen.

1v1 Nassau

One player vs another. Multiple matchups can run simultaneously.

Auto-Press

When a game reaches −2 margin, a press starts from the next hole. Presses can be pressed. All press history shown in the Games tab.

The International (The Big I)

Available after Hole 17 to the losing team. A single Hole 18 can cancel, double, or leave unchanged the entire pot.

  • Losing team wins Hole 18 → pot voided.
  • Leading team wins → pot doubles.
  • Halved → pot stays.

The app won't let you forget to call it: the first time you reach hole 18 with an eligible match, a full-screen reminder appears with two clear choices — go call it now, or confirm no International this round. If you later go back and edit a score on any earlier hole, the reminder automatically re-arms and will prompt you again next time you reach hole 18.

Whenever you're on the 💵 Games screen mid-round, a floating "⛳ Return to Scorecard" button stays pinned to the bottom-right of the screen — tap it any time to jump straight back to scoring.

5. The 6-Point Game

Three-player game. Each hole: low net wins 4 pts, second wins 2, third wins 0. Points convert to dollars at round end. Ties for low give 3 pts each.

7. Skins

Each hole is its own contest. Win outright → win the skin. Tie → skin carries over. Select players, set dollars per skin, choose Gross or Net. After 18: Void (default) or Last Hole Sweeps.

With five players at $5/skin, three carried skins on a hole is worth $15 to the winner.

8. Vegas

Two-against-two. Each team's scores combine low digit first (4 and 5 = 45). Teams play the difference per point. The Flip: an opponent birdie flips your number so the high digit leads (45 → 54). Requires 4 or 5 players. Multiple pairings can run simultaneously.

9. The Snake 3-Putt Money Game

Settles every 3 holes. Whoever holds the snake at holes 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18 pays each other participant the bet amount. Enable in the 🐍 Snake accordion, set bet per player, select participants.

10. Match Cards

Play 1v1 Nassau against golfers in OTHER groups. Your scores tracked live — opponents' scores entered later in the clubhouse. The ⚔️ Match Cards round lands in History with a purple border and 📝 Score Round pill when your round finishes.

12. The Course Picker

📋 Course Info

Tap 📋 Info on any course row: live weather, your history there (best, avg, lifetime money, last 12 scores vs par), and full 18-hole scorecard with all tee yardages, ratings and slopes.

Tee Colours

Tee pills are colour-coded. Combo tees (Black/Gold, Blue/White, etc.) show as a split diagonal gradient.

Round History on Course Rows

Each course row shows rounds played and last 3 gross scores — visible before you even select the course.

Weather Sources

The Setup screen top-right always shows your home weather. The scorecard weather strip during a round always shows weather for the course being played, regardless of your home city.

Every course shows its own live weather, independently. This is separate from your Home City setting — open the course picker and every single course row (all 63) fetches and displays its own current temperature, wind, and conditions, right where it is, not where you are. Edmonton-area courses share one efficient weather call; every other course fetches in small background batches so the list never feels slow to open.

  • Home City left blank or set to "Edmonton" — uses a personal WeatherLink station at Derrick.
  • Any other Home City — uses live Open-Meteo weather for that city.

13. Round History

Tap 📜 History in the bottom nav. A floating ‹ Return button appears as you scroll.

  • Saving — tap 💾 Save Round, or rounds save automatically on New Round.
  • Locking — tap 🔓 to lock; hold 🔒 for 2 seconds to unlock.
  • Editing — tap ✏️ Edit on any completed round.
  • Pausing — tap ⚙️ End Round Early → 💾 Save & Pause Round. Resume from History.

14. Backup & Restore

Tap 💾 Backup / Data in the bottom action bar.

  • 📤 Export Backup — saves to Files, iCloud, or email. Named "Pards-Golf-Backup-Jun-28-2026.json."
  • 📥 Import Backup — restores from a previously exported file.
Export a backup regularly. iOS can wipe PWA storage when you remove the Home Screen icon, run low on storage, or during some iOS updates.

A floating "‹ Return" button stays pinned to the bottom-right of the Backup screen, same as on Games and Round History — tap it any time to head back without scrolling up first.

15. Starting a New Round

Tap 🔄 New Round in the bottom nav. Hold the confirmation button for 2 seconds. Rounds in progress show a red ⚠️ dialog — hold to abandon, release to cancel.

16. Manage My Players

Tap ⚙️ Manage My Players (below Load My Favourites on Setup). A search bar floats at the bottom of the screen — type a few letters and a live counter above it shows how many players match (e.g. "6 players found"), narrowing as you type. Tap the ✕ inside the search bar to clear it instantly. Players are sorted with "Me" first, then ⭐ Favourites, then alphabetically.

  • 🔄 Refresh All Handicaps — fetches the latest Golf Canada Index for every saved player with a Golf Canada ID on file, one at a time, showing live progress (e.g. "Fetching 14 of 78…"). Players without a Golf Canada ID are skipped automatically.
  • Mark yourself as "Me" — auto-fills Player 1 every round. If "Me" is already set, you'll get a confirmation prompt before switching to a different person.
  • Star players to mark as Favourites.
  • Tee Deck — tap the coloured tee button to pick a preferred tee for the current course from a modal list.
  • Score Wheel Default — tap the button to set Par, Bogey, or Double as the starting point on the score entry wheel.
  • 🌤️ Home City (Me only) — sets which city's weather shows on the Setup screen. Leave blank or set to Edmonton to use your personal WeatherLink station; any other city uses live weather from Open-Meteo.
  • ⛳ Home Course (Me only) — sets your default course. It always appears first in the course picker and is selected automatically on every new round.
  • Automatic tee matching — every saved player's Derrick tee deck (Deck 1 through Deck 4) maps to a target slope and yardage combination, and the app automatically picks the closest-matching tee at every other course in the database. Manually picking a different tee for a player at a specific course always takes priority and is never overwritten.
  • Edit names, handicaps, Golf Canada IDs, tee preferences.

A gold Done ✓ button floats in the top-right corner so you can close the overlay from anywhere while scrolling.

The tee you set in "Tee Deck" is remembered per course, per player — not just one global preference. Switch to a different course in the picker, open Manage My Players, and set that player's tee there; the app remembers that exact tee for that exact course forever, while your tee at every other course stays untouched. Play 20 different courses with 20 different favourite tees, all remembered independently.

18. Troubleshooting

My round disappeared 🏌️

Use 💾 Save & Pause Round before ending early. Without saving, data cannot be recovered.

The app looks outdated 🤔

Tap Update App. If that doesn't work, clear Safari site data for bdcomeau.github.io, force-close, reopen.

Pop-ups aren't appearing 😶

Run from the Home Screen icon, not a Safari tab.

Player data or history disappeared 😱

Likely caused by "Remove App." Restore from a backup file if available. Always export backups regularly.

A player left mid-round 🚑

Tap ⚙️ Mid-Round Match & Player Settings and remove them there.

A score or game looks wrong 🧮

  • Handicaps editable mid-round in ⚙️ Mid-Round settings.
  • Scores correctable at any time — all totals recalculate instantly.
  • Wrong tee: go to Manage My Players → edit contact → set preferred tee.

Tee box button not responding 📱

Tap anywhere on the dark overlay to dismiss, then try again.

Weather not showing 🌦️

You may be offline. The app works fine without weather.

Handicap strokes wrong ✏️

Formula: Index × (Slope ÷ 113) + (Course Rating − Par), rounded to nearest whole number. Check index, tee, and course are all correct.

History lock not working 🔒

Hold for the full 2 seconds without moving your finger — the icon fades as you hold.
