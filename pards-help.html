⛳ 1 · Overview

Pards Golf is an offline-first mobile scorecard and golf betting app. It tracks strokes, calculates handicapped net scores, runs Nassau matches, Skins, Vegas, Stableford, Six-Point, Snake, Internationals, and Birdie Juice — all without needing an internet connection.

The app works as a saved HTML file on your phone. Open it in Safari, then use Share → Add to Home Screen to install it as an icon on your home screen so it launches full-screen.

📱 Five Screens
  • Scorecard — enter strokes hole by hole, see running totals
  • Games — view live Nassau cards, Skins, running tallies for all active bets
  • Birdie Juice — track the Birdie Juice pot and contributions
  • History — browse past rounds, view detailed cards, share / print
  • New Round — start fresh (prompts to save the current round first)
⚙️ Setup Screen

Accessed by tapping the Pards Golf logo or header. Choose course, date, players, tees, handicap indexes, and which games to play. The setup screen is also where you manage contacts and access Backup & Restore.

⚙️ 2 · Setting Up a Round
Step-by-Step
  1. Choose a course — tap the course name at the top to open the course picker. 38 courses are built in. Derrick Golf & Winter Club is always pinned first; others are alphabetical.
  2. Set number of players — tap 2, 3, or 4.
  3. Add players — tap each player slot. Type a name or pick from your Favourites list. The app fills in their handicap index, tee deck, and Golf Canada ID automatically.
  4. Choose tees — each player has a tee dropdown. The app auto-selects the tee closest to their Derrick deck slope when switching courses.
  5. Set handicap indexes — pre-filled from Favourites. Adjust if needed.
  6. Choose games — tap Games in the bottom nav to pick Nassau, Skins, Vegas, etc. Set stakes for each game.
  7. Set start hole — if your group starts on hole 10 (shotgun), tap the start hole selector.
  8. Tap Start Round →
🏟️ Shotgun Starts

Select any starting hole on the setup screen. The scorecard cycles from that hole through 18 and wraps around. Nassau front/back/18 and all game calculations adjust automatically.

⚙️ Mid-Round Changes

Tap ⚙️ Mid-Round Match & Player Settings on the Scorecard screen to add or remove games, adjust presses, or change players after the round has started.

💡 Tip Load Favourites first — tap Load Favourites on the setup screen to quickly populate your usual group from saved contacts.
📋 3 · Scorecard
Entering Scores

Tap a player's cell on the current hole to enter their gross score. Use the + / − buttons or tap a number directly. The scorecard advances to the next hole automatically after all players are entered (if Auto-Advance is on).

What You See
  • Gross score — what the player actually shot
  • Net score — gross minus strokes received on that hole (shown below gross in smaller text)
  • Running total — cumulative gross to par, updated after each hole
  • Hole SI — stroke index (handicap difficulty) shown in the hole header row
Colour Coding
  • Green circle — birdie (gross score in a single green circle)
  • Double green circle — eagle or better
  • No circle — par or bogey
  • Square outline — double bogey or worse
🖨️ Share / Print

Tap the printer icon at the top right of the Scorecard screen to generate a shareable summary — a full scorecard with all player scores, net scores, and game results. Uses the iOS Share Sheet so you can AirDrop, email, or save to Files.

📝 Note The scorecard saves automatically as you enter each score. If you close the app and reopen it, your round is exactly where you left it.
💵 4 · Games & Betting

Tap Games in the bottom nav during a round to see the live games sheet. All games run simultaneously and are settled automatically at round end.

Adding Games

Games are set up before the round on the Setup screen, or mid-round via ⚙️ Mid-Round Match & Player Settings. Tap any game tile to configure it — choose pairings, stakes, and options.

Money Display
  • $ — settled amount (round complete or hole closed)
  • ~$ — projected amount based on current scores (live, may change)
  • Green = winning · Red = losing
🏆 5 · Nassau

Nassau is the primary game — three separate bets: Front 9, Back 9, and 18 holes. Each is won by the side with the lower net score over that stretch.

How It Works
  • Two sides compete (e.g. A&B vs C&D, or A vs B in a singles match)
  • Net scores are used (gross minus handicap strokes)
  • Each of the three bets pays independently — you can win the front, lose the back, win the 18
  • Stakes are set per bet (e.g. $5 Nassau = $5 front, $5 back, $5 18-hole)
Presses

A press is a new side-bet that triggers automatically when a side goes 2-down. It starts on the next hole and runs to the end of that segment (front or back), paying the same stake as the original bet.

  • Presses trigger automatically — no manual calling needed
  • Multiple presses can stack if a side keeps falling behind
  • Each press pays independently
  • The Nassau card scrolls right to show all active presses
Nassau Card

The Games screen shows a full Nassau scorecard with player names, adjusted handicaps, running hole-by-hole results, and the F9 / B9 / 18 / Press columns. A mid-round result banner always shows ("so far" for in-progress amounts).

💡 Tip The Nassau card can be printed/shared from the History screen after the round — it shows the full breakdown including all presses and final dollar amounts.
💀 6 · Skins

Skins is a hole-by-hole competition. The player (or team) with the lowest net score on a hole wins the skin for that hole. If two or more players tie, the skin carries over to the next hole.

Options
  • Gross or Net — choose whether handicap strokes apply
  • Carryover — tied holes roll the pot into the next hole
  • Per-hole stake — each skin worth the set amount (e.g. $2/skin)
  • Group Skins — all players in one pot, or paired team skins
🎲 7 · Other Games
🎰 Vegas

Two teams of two. Each team's scores combine into a two-digit number, lower score first (e.g. 4 and 5 = 45). The team with the lower number wins the difference × the stake. Optional: if the opposing team makes a birdie, your number flips to high-first (45 → 54). The flip-on-birdie option can be turned on or off in setup.

📊 Stableford

Points-based scoring: Eagle = 4pts, Birdie = 3pts, Par = 2pts, Bogey = 1pt, Double bogey or worse = 0pts. Uses net scores. Most points wins. Great for keeping everyone engaged even after a bad stretch.

6️⃣ Six Point

Requires exactly 3 players selected. Each hole has 6 points up for grabs — distributed by net score finish. The lowest-scoring player on each hole drops to 0 points. Uses net scores (handicap applied off the low player). Cumulative over 18 holes — highest total wins, loser pays winner the points difference × the stake.

🐍 Snake

The player who three-putts picks up the snake. They hold it until someone else three-putts. Whoever is holding the snake at the end of the round pays the pot. Multiple snakes can be in play simultaneously.

🌍 The International

A side-bet called mid-round between two Nassau matchups. When one side is losing their Nassau match, they can challenge the other side to "The International" — a separate all-in bet settled at round end. Called from the Games screen when available. The option appears automatically when the conditions are right.

📋 Scores Only

No betting — just track gross and net scores for all players. Useful for casual rounds or when you just want a clean scorecard to share.

🐦🥃 8 · Birdie Juice

Birdie Juice is a group pot funded by birdies. Every birdie made by any player contributes a set amount to the pot. The pot is paid out to the player who makes the last birdie of the round (or split among all birdie makers, depending on your rules).

How to Use It
  1. Enable Birdie Juice from the Games menu and set the per-birdie contribution amount.
  2. As players make birdies during the round, they are logged automatically from the scorecard.
  3. Tap the Birdie Juice tab to see the current pot size, who's contributed, and who holds it.
  4. At round end, the pot is settled and included in the money summary.
📐 9 · Handicaps
Course Handicap Calculation

Pards Golf calculates each player's course handicap using the standard USGA formula:

Course HCP = Index × (Slope ÷ 113) + (Rating − Par)

This is calculated automatically from the player's handicap index and the tee they're playing. The result is rounded to the nearest whole number.

Stroke Allocation

Course handicap strokes are allocated by hole SI (Stroke Index). A player receiving 10 strokes gets one stroke on the 10 hardest holes (SI 1–10). A player receiving 19 strokes gets a double stroke on SI 1 and one stroke on SI 2–18.

Tee Decks (Derrick)

At Derrick Golf & Winter Club, tees are named by deck (Deck 1 through Deck 4) rather than colour. When you switch to another course, the app automatically selects the tee with the closest slope rating to your Derrick deck:

  • Deck 1 (slope 121) → hardest comparable tee
  • Deck 2 (slope 119) → mid-difficulty tee
  • Deck 3 (slope 117) → easier tee
  • Deck 4 (slope 109) → softest available tee
Handicap Index Updates

Player handicap indexes are stored in Contacts. To update an index: go to Setup → tap the player → edit their index. The change is saved to their contact record and applies to all future rounds.

📝 Note Handicap indexes are entered manually. Pards Golf does not connect to Golf Canada or any external handicap system — you need to enter the current index yourself.

👥 11 · Contacts & Favourites
What's Stored Per Contact
  • Name
  • Handicap index
  • Golf Canada ID (for handicap verification)
  • Default tee deck (Derrick)
  • Preferred tee per course (set automatically or manually)
  • Favourite flag
  • Me flag (marks your own record for lifetime money totals)
Managing Contacts
  1. On the Setup screen, tap Manage Favourites.
  2. Tap any contact to edit their name, index, Golf Canada ID, or tee preferences.
  3. Toggle the ⭐ to mark as a favourite (appears at the top of lists).
  4. Tap + Add Contact to create a new one.
Tee Preferences Per Course

Every contact stores their preferred tee for every course. When you switch courses, the app auto-selects the tee closest to their Derrick deck slope. You can manually override this at any time — the manual choice is saved and persists for future rounds at that course.

Lifetime Money Totals

Mark your own record with the Me flag. The Setup screen and History screen will show your running lifetime money total across all rounds in the app.

📜 12 · Round History

Tap History in the bottom nav to browse all completed rounds. Rounds are sorted newest first.

Each History Entry Shows
  • Date and course
  • Players and their scores
  • Money won/lost per player
  • Weather at start and end
Round Detail

Tap any round to open a full read-only view. From there you can:

  • 🖨️ Share / Print — generates a full scorecard + Nassau card as a shareable page
  • ✏️ Edit — correct scores or money after the fact
  • ▶ Resume — if the round was saved mid-way, resume entering scores
  • 🗑 Delete — permanently remove the round (hold for 2 seconds to confirm)
💾 13 · Backup & Restore

Go to Setup → Backup (tap the 💾 icon in the setup action bar at the bottom of the setup screen).

Export Backup

Tap 📤 Export Backup. The iOS Share Sheet opens — save the .json file to Files, iCloud, or email it to yourself. The file contains all contacts, all round history, and your current round in progress.

⚠️ Important Reinstalling the app or clearing Safari website data wipes all stored data. Keep a recent backup in iCloud or email. The app reminds you to back up after every few rounds.
Import / Restore Backup

Tap 📥 Import Backup, select a previously exported .json file. This completely replaces the current app data with the backup — use with care.

💡 Tip Name your backup files with a date so you can find the right one easily. The app auto-names them like Pards-Golf-Backup-Jun-27-2026-1306.json.
🔄 14 · Import from Nassau Golf

If you were previously using the Nassau Golf app (the predecessor to Pards Golf), you can import your contacts and round history.

How to Import
  1. Open Nassau Golf on your phone.
  2. Go to Backup → Export Backup and save the file to your Files app.
  3. Open Pards Golf and go to Backup & Restore.
  4. Tap 📲 Select Nassau Golf Backup File and choose the file you just saved.
  5. The app merges your contacts and history — existing Pards data is never overwritten.
What Gets Imported
  • Contacts — names, handicap indexes, Golf Canada IDs, deck preferences (DECK1–4 → Deck 1–4). Duplicates (matching name or ID) are skipped.
  • History — all completed rounds, tagged as Derrick Golf rounds (Nassau Golf was Derrick-only). Duplicate rounds (same date) are skipped.
  • Tee migration — after import, the app automatically assigns the best-matching tee on every course for each imported contact based on their Derrick deck.
📝 Note iOS keeps each local HTML file in its own sandboxed storage, so the import must go via an exported file — the two apps cannot read each other's data directly.
💡 15 · Tips & Troubleshooting
App Won't Update on My Phone

Tap Update App in the setup action bar at the bottom of the Setup screen. If that doesn't work, close Safari completely, reopen the file, and try again. The app uses a service worker cache — the update button forces it to clear the cache and reload the latest version.

Lost My Data

Safari may clear website data if storage is low or if "Clear History and Website Data" was used. Always keep a recent backup. Go to Backup → Export Backup after each round.

Scorecard Shows Wrong Handicap Strokes

Check that: (1) the player's handicap index is correct, (2) the correct tee is selected (slope affects course handicap), and (3) you're on the right course. Course handicap = Index × (Slope ÷ 113) + (Rating − Par).

Add to Home Screen (iPhone)
  1. Open pards-golf.html in Safari.
  2. Tap the Share button (rectangle with arrow pointing up).
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Name it "Pards Golf" and tap Add.

The app now launches full-screen from your home screen, just like a native app.

Two Players Tied on a Nassau Hole

Ties are a halve — neither side wins the hole. If using net scores, a halved hole still counts as a halve even if gross scores differ.

Wrong Course Auto-Selected on Switch

The app picks the tee closest to the player's Derrick deck slope. If a player's deck preference isn't set, it defaults to Deck 2 equivalent (slope ~119). Go to Manage Favourites → edit the contact → set their Derrick deck.
